Common types of personal loans

Hello friend!

Debt Consolidation- You can use this type of loan if you have a large amount of debt you want to pay off your credit cards or other personal loans.
Mortgage refinancing – Mortgage refinancing is commonly used to lower the interest rate on your current home loan.
Auto refinancing- You can refinance your automobile if you’d like to get a lower IR or APR on your auto loan.
Vacation- You can apply for this type of loan if you need money for a vacation or business trip.
Business- Business loans are most commonly used for starting small businesses. There are many different kinds of business loans and the one’s you qualify for depend on incoming capital and or capital needed.
Moving- You can apply for a moving loan if you need to move and the company you work for doesn’t offer relocation reimbursement.
Medical- You can take advantage of a medical loan if you need to pay for certain medical bills.

Common documents needed for approval

W2 - This tax form shows your income from the previous year. Credit specialists often consider this amount and determine whether you will be able to pay the loan back in sufficient time.
Pay stubs- You receive this from your employer bi-weekly or monthly, it depends on who you work for. Credit specialists can use pay stubs to calculate your income on a monthly or bi-weekly basis in order to determine whether you're eligible for a loan.
Tax forms - There are many different types of tax forms that you can use to file your taxes. The forms that you use often change based on the types of taxes you file. If you’ve filed with Credit Karma tax, you can find your forms from the previous year here. If you’re not a W2 employee then the credit specialists will typically ask to see a 1099 or your schedule C to verify self-employment.
4506-T - This is a unique form that’s sent to the federal government in order to verify the taxes you sent to the lending company matches what the government has on file for you.
Identification documents - There are many types of identification documents. Some of the more common ones are: Driver’s license, Passport and Social Security Card.
Utility Bill - There are various types of utility bills, such as: electricity, water, gas, etc. They are often used to verify that your home address matches the address you have on file with the lending company.

What is an adverse action?

An adverse action is a notice given to you by the lending company which gives you a reason for being denied a loan or credit. No need to worry if you’ve received one of these, lenders send them to explain why you weren’t pre-qualified.

Why did I receive this?

Due to FCRA (Fair Credit Reporting Act) all lending companies have to let you know why you weren’t qualified. The notice has to be given orally, written, or in an electronic format. You can get an Adverse Action if you need to increase your credit score, send in an incomplete application, or have a limited credit history. By law a company must notify you within 30 days of receiving a credit application.

I was rejected, now what?

The lender looks at multiple factors to determine the eligibility of a borrower. If you haven’t approved it is not the end of the road. You also might consider credit unions, banks, or other online lending companies. You can also get in contact with the credit bureau the lender used and request a free copy of your credit report. It is best to look over your credit reports for any discrepancies and dispute errors with the bureau.
